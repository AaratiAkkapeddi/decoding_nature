Reading the City

By Ernest

Reading the City is an interactive data-visualization project that uses the visual language of the NYC subway map to explore literacy across the city. Instead of presenting statistics as a static chart, the project turns data into a navigable civic landscape where subway lines, boroughs, movement, and neighborhood information become part of the visual system. Viewers are invited to read the city as a map, a dataset, and a story about access.

Ernest Estimé is a dreamer by default, fueled by faith and driven by purpose in creative pursuits. His art is interdisciplinary. The prose of his artistry often intersects and curiously engages the relationship between cities, systems, memory, and visual culture. Like hip-hop, an element of its indelible influence on generations is due to a particular style in production; The Sample. And like a sample, Ern likes starting with familiar public languages — maps, sports graphics, transit signs, comics, music, street photography — then reworking them into new ways of seeing power, place, and everyday life. “I’m interested in making work that feels visually immediate while carrying deeper questions about community, identity, and how information shapes the way we move through the world.”

WHAT YOU ARE LOOKING AT

Each subway station is paired with a New York City Community School District. The map turns every route into a reading-data corridor, showing literacy proficiency, district context, income, English learner share, and whether the data is verified or estimated.

HOW TO READ IT

Hover to preview a station. Click to lock its data into the right panel. Click again to release it. Animated flows suggest movement, student circulation, and civic urgency across the citywide system.

NOTES + INSIGHTS
▮▮▮

Literacy Varies Across Boroughs

Rate gaps widen as routes move farther from central hubs and high-proficiency districts.

●●●

Transfers Create Opportunity

Major interchange stations become natural zones for outreach, intervention, and citywide literacy support.

▭

Data Informs Action

Verified rates, income, and English learner share expose where support is most urgent.

◉

Stronger System, Stronger Students

A citywide literacy system should move resources as deliberately as trains move riders.
